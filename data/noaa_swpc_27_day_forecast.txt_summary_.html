Summary: The 27-day Space Weather Outlook, issued on January 27, 2025, by NOAA's Space Weather Prediction Center provides forecasts for solar radio flux, planetary A index, and the largest Kp index from January 27 to February 22, 2025. These parameters are critical in understanding solar activity and its effects on Earth.

Findings & Results: - The solar radio flux, measured at 10.7 cm (an indicator of solar activity), ranged from 140 to 170 units throughout the forecast period, showing moderate solar activity with a slight increase towards the end. - The planetary A index, which reflects geomagnetic activity, varied between 5 and 20, suggesting occasional geomagnetic disturbances primarily between February 2-3 and February 11-13. - The largest Kp index, a measure of geomagnetic storm intensity on a scale from 0 to 9, ranged from 2 to 5. A Kp index of 5, indicating a minor geomagnetic storm, is expected on February 2, February 11-13.

Implications: These predictions highlight periods of increased geomagnetic activity, which can affect satellite operations, GPS accuracy, and power grid stability on Earth. The forecast suggests monitoring these dates for potential impacts and preparing mitigation strategies where necessary.

Terminology: - Radio Flux (10.7 cm): A measure of solar emissions at a wavelength of 10.7 centimeters; higher values indicate more intense solar activity. - Planetary A Index: A daily average of the geomagnetic activity derived from magnetometer readings worldwide. Larger values indicate higher geomagnetic activity. - Kp Index: A global geomagnetic activity index measured on a scale of 0 to 9, where higher numbers indicate stronger geomagnetic storms.

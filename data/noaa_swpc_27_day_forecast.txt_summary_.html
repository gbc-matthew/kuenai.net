Issued Date

2025-02-24

Summary

The 27-day Space Weather Outlook, issued by the NOAA's Space Weather Prediction Center on February 24, 2025, provides a forecast of solar radio flux, planetary A index, and the largest Kp index over a period extending from February 24, 2025, to March 22, 2025. These indices help predict space weather, which can have significant impacts on Earth and human activities, particularly in communication systems and satellite operations.

Findings & Results

  • Radio Flux: Generally decreasing trend from 210 solar flux units on February 24 to 200 on March 22, with fluctuations between 155 and 210.

  • Planetary A Index: Peaks of 25 and 30 towards March 9-14, indicating periods of higher geomagnetic activity.

  • Largest Kp Index: Values rise to 5 on March 9, 12, 13, and 14, suggesting moderate geomagnetic storms on these days.

Implications

  • Communication Disruptions: Higher Kp indices can cause disruptions in radio communications, particularly affecting HF radio bands.

  • Navigation Systems: Geomagnetic activity peaks can impact GNSS (Global Navigation Satellite Systems) accuracy and reliability.

  • Power Systems: Increased geomagnetic activity could strain power grids, possibly leading to outages if storm levels are high enough.

Terminology

  • Radio Flux (10.7 cm): A measure of solar emissions, indicating solar activity levels. Higher values often correspond to increased solar radiation effects.

  • Planetary A Index: An average measure of geomagnetic activity. Higher values suggest more intense geomagnetic conditions.

  • Kp Index: A scale of 0 to 9, used to quantify disturbances in the Earth's magnetic field. A Kp value of 5 or above indicates a geomagnetic storm.

This outlook helps in preparing for and mitigating potential impacts of space weather phenomena on technological systems and infrastructure.

Issued Date

2025 Feb 24 1230 UTC

Summary

The Space Weather Prediction Center reports high solar activity levels characterized by significant solar flares and enhanced solar wind parameters. Notably, a strong X2.0 flare was observed, with various regions on the Sun contributing to solar flare events. Despite increased solar activity, no Earth-directed Coronal Mass Ejections (CMEs) are detected, and geomagnetic effects on Earth remain mild.

Findings & Results

  • Solar Activity: The Sun produced a significant X2.0 (R3-Strong) solar flare, indicating high solar activity. Additional smaller flares (M1.3 and R1-Minor) were observed.
  • Solar Flare Regions: Regions 4001, 3998, and 4000 were active, with each contributing to solar flare events.
  • Particle Flux: The greater than 10 MeV proton flux increased but remained below the minor storm levels. Electron flux levels fluctuated between normal to moderate.
  • Solar Wind: Enhanced solar wind parameters were noted post-23/2100 UTC, linked to a CME that departed the Sun on 19 Feb. However, solar wind speeds were relatively low at ~300-375 km/s.
  • Geomagnetic Activity: The geomagnetic field remained mostly quiet to unsettled, indicating minimal impact on Earth's magnetosphere.

Implications

  • Solar Flares: There is a likelihood of continued minor to moderate solar flare activity, with chances of strong flares potentially impacting satellite operations and communications on Earth.
  • Proton and Electron Flux: While current particle flux levels pose no immediate threat, there's a possibility of reaching minor storm levels, which can impact satellite health and polar aviation.
  • Geomagnetic Activity: The geomagnetic activity forecast indicates quiet to unsettled conditions, suggesting minimal disturbance to technological systems relying on Earth's magnetosphere.

Terminology

  • Solar Flare: A sudden eruption of intense high-energy radiation from the sun's surface.
  • Coronal Mass Ejection (CME): A significant release of plasma and magnetic field from the solar corona.
  • Proton Flux: A measure of the number of solar protons passing through a given area, affecting space-based technologies.
  • Solar Wind: A stream of charged particles (mostly electrons and protons) emitted by the sun.
  • Geomagnetic Field: The magnetic field surrounding Earth, which protects against solar and cosmic radiation.
  • Negative Polarity CH HSS: Coronal Hole High-Speed Stream with a northward magnetic field orientation, impacting Earth's magnetosphere.
